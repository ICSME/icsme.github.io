To rebut or not to rebut? This is the question :)

The following was mined from the easychair data of the technical track of ICSME 2022. 35 papers were selected for rebuttal, which led to 6 rating changes. With 3 down and 4 up the average change was only +1/7 of a point:

before R after
-1 -1 1 R 1 -> -1
2 -1 1 R -1 -> 1
2 -1 -1 2 -> 1 R 1 -> 0 -1 -> 0
2 1 -1 R 1 -> -1
2 2 -1 R -1 -> 1
-2 1 -2 R -2 -> -1

In summary, from an economic or engineering perspective the rebuttal process is not worth the cost.

"Any final thoughts or comments on having an author response?"

These break down into two groups, people interested in saving time and thus who are supportive of focusing the effort, and people interested in maximizing the available information regardless of the cost. This second group also include a few who confuse equity with equality, which suggests the need for further refinement of the presentation of the process.

Kudos to the PC chairs for innovating on this aspect of the ICSME review process!
